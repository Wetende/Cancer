Breast Anatomy

Understanding breast anatomy is crucial for comprehending breast cancer. The breast consists of milk-producing glands (lobules), ducts that carry milk to the nipple, and supportive tissue.

Warning Signs and Symptoms

Warning Signs in Women

  • New lump in the breast or underarm
  • Thickening or swelling of part of the breast
  • Irritation or dimpling of breast skin
  • Redness or flaky skin in the nipple area
  • Pulling in of the nipple
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk
  • Any change in the size or shape of the breast
  • Pain in any area of the breast
Learn More

Warning Signs in Men

  • Lump or swelling in the breast, chest, or underarm area
  • Skin dimpling or puckering
  • Nipple turning inward
  • Redness or scaling of nipple or breast skin
  • Discharge from the nipple
