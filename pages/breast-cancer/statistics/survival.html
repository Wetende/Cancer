Overview

Breast cancer survival rates have improved significantly over the past few decades due to earlier detection and advances in treatment. Here are some key statistics:

90%

5-year relative survival rate for all stages combined

99%

5-year survival rate for localized breast cancer

3.8M

Breast cancer survivors in the United States

Survival Rates by Stage

Survival rates vary significantly depending on the stage at diagnosis:

Stage 5-Year Relative Survival Rate Description
Stage 0 (DCIS) Nearly 100% Non-invasive, contained within the milk ducts
Stage I 99% Early-stage invasive cancer
Stage II 85% Cancer has grown but remains localized
Stage III 70% Locally advanced cancer
Stage IV 28% Metastatic cancer that has spread to other parts of the body

Survival Trends

Breast cancer survival rates have been improving over time due to several factors:

Key Improvements

  • Death rates have dropped 43% from 1989 to 2020
  • Earlier detection through regular screening
  • More effective treatments
  • Better understanding of different breast cancer types
  • Improved targeted therapies

Factors Affecting Survival

Several factors can influence breast cancer survival rates:

Biological Factors

  • Cancer stage at diagnosis
  • Tumor grade and size
  • Hormone receptor status
  • HER2 status
  • Rate of cell growth

Treatment Factors

  • Type of treatment received
  • Response to treatment
  • Access to quality healthcare
  • Adherence to treatment plan

Personal Factors

  • Age at diagnosis
  • Overall health status
  • Lifestyle factors
  • Support system

Demographics and Survival

Survival rates can vary among different demographic groups:

Key Demographic Trends

  • Higher survival rates in women diagnosed at younger ages
  • Variations in survival rates among different racial and ethnic groups
  • Geographic differences in survival rates
  • Socioeconomic factors affecting access to care and outcomes
