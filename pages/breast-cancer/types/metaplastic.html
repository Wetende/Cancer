Overview

Metaplastic breast cancer (MBC) is a rare and aggressive form of breast cancer that accounts for less than 1% of all breast cancers. It's characterized by cells that have transformed from one type to another, making it more complex to treat than typical breast cancers.

Important Notice

Metaplastic breast cancer often grows quickly and may be less responsive to standard breast cancer treatments. Early detection and specialized treatment are crucial.

Types of Metaplastic Breast Cancer

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Cancer cells resemble the flat cells that line the skin and other organs.

Spindle Cell Carcinoma

Cancer cells have a long, spindle-shaped appearance.

Matrix-Producing Carcinoma

Cancer cells produce cartilage and/or bone tissue.

Signs and Symptoms

Metaplastic breast cancer often presents as:

  • A firm, rapidly growing breast lump
  • Large tumor size at diagnosis
  • Changes in breast size or shape
  • Skin changes over the breast
  • Breast pain or tenderness
  • Nipple changes or discharge

Diagnosis

Diagnosing metaplastic breast cancer requires:

  • Physical examination
  • Diagnostic mammogram and ultrasound
  • Breast MRI
  • Core needle biopsy
  • Specialized pathology testing
  • Additional imaging to check for spread

Treatment Options

Treatment for metaplastic breast cancer typically involves a combination of approaches:

Chemotherapy

Often given before surgery (neoadjuvant) to shrink the tumor, though response rates may be lower than with other types of breast cancer.

Surgery

Usually mastectomy due to tumor size and aggressive nature, though breast-conserving surgery may be possible in some cases.

Radiation

Often recommended after surgery to reduce the risk of local recurrence.

Clinical Trials

Due to its rarity and aggressive nature, participation in clinical trials may be recommended for access to new treatments.

Prognosis

The prognosis for metaplastic breast cancer depends on several factors:

  • Stage at diagnosis
  • Size of the tumor
  • Whether the cancer has spread
  • Response to treatment
  • Overall health and age
  • Specific type of metaplastic breast cancer
