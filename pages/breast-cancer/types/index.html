Breast Cancer Types Overview

Understanding Breast Cancer Classifications

Breast cancer is not a single disease but rather a group of diseases that differ in how they grow and spread, how they appear on imaging tests, and how they respond to different treatments. Understanding the specific type of breast cancer is crucial for determining the most effective treatment approach.

Breast cancers are generally categorized based on:

  • Where in the breast they start (ductal vs. lobular)
  • Whether they have spread beyond their origin (non-invasive vs. invasive)
  • Their molecular characteristics (hormone receptor status, HER2 status)
  • Their appearance under a microscope (histologic type)

Non-invasive Breast Cancers

Non-invasive breast cancers are confined to the ducts or lobules and have not spread to surrounding tissues.

Ductal Carcinoma In Situ (DCIS)

A non-invasive breast cancer where abnormal cells are found in the lining of the breast milk ducts. DCIS is considered stage 0 breast cancer and is highly treatable, but if left untreated, it may progress to invasive cancer.

Learn About DCIS

Invasive Breast Cancers

Invasive breast cancers have spread from the milk ducts or lobules to surrounding breast tissues and may spread to lymph nodes or other parts of the body.

Invasive Breast Cancer

Includes invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC) and invasive lobular carcinoma (ILC). These cancers have broken through the wall of the duct or lobule and invaded surrounding breast tissue, with the potential to spread to lymph nodes and other parts of the body.

Learn About Invasive Breast Cancer

Inflammatory Breast Cancer

A rare and aggressive type of breast cancer that causes the breast to become red, swollen, and warm. It often doesn't cause a distinct lump and can be mistaken for an infection, leading to delayed diagnosis.

Learn About Inflammatory Breast Cancer

Other Types of Breast Cancer

Several less common types of breast cancer have unique characteristics and treatment considerations.

Metaplastic Breast Cancer

A rare form of invasive breast cancer where cells in the duct take on characteristics of other types of tissues, such as skin or bone. It tends to be aggressive and less responsive to standard treatments.

Learn About Metaplastic Breast Cancer

Male Breast Cancer

Breast cancer that occurs in men. Although rare (less than 1% of all breast cancers), men can develop breast cancer and should be aware of the symptoms.

Learn About Male Breast Cancer

Paget's Disease of the Breast

A rare type of breast cancer that begins in the ducts and spreads to the nipple and areola. It often presents as scaly, red, itchy patches on the nipple that may resemble eczema or dermatitis.

Learn About Paget's Disease
