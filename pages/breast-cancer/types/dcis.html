Overview

What is DCIS?

Ductal Carcinoma In Situ (DCIS) is a non-invasive breast cancer where abnormal cells are found in the lining of the breast milk ducts. The term "in situ" means that the abnormal cells remain in their original place and haven't spread to surrounding breast tissue.

Signs & Symptoms

Common Symptoms

  • DCIS usually has no signs or symptoms
  • Most cases are detected through mammogram screening
  • May appear as tiny spots of calcium (microcalcifications) on mammograms
  • Rarely, may cause a breast lump or nipple discharge

Diagnosis

Diagnostic Methods

  • Mammogram - Primary screening tool
  • Breast biopsy - Confirms diagnosis
  • MRI - May be used in specific cases
  • Pathology testing - Determines DCIS grade

Treatment Options

Surgery

  • Lumpectomy (breast-conserving)
  • Mastectomy (full breast removal)
  • Reconstruction options available

Radiation Therapy

  • Often follows lumpectomy
  • Reduces recurrence risk
  • Targeted treatment area

Hormone Therapy

  • For hormone-receptor-positive DCIS
  • Tamoxifen or aromatase inhibitors
  • Reduces recurrence risk

Risk Factors

Common Risk Factors

  • Age over 50
  • Family history of breast cancer
  • Previous breast biopsies
  • Dense breast tissue
  • Early onset of menstruation
  • Late menopause
  • Never having children
  • First pregnancy after age 30
  • Hormone replacement therapy
  • Obesity

Prevention

Preventive Measures

  • Regular mammogram screenings
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Regular physical activity
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Discuss risk factors with healthcare provider
