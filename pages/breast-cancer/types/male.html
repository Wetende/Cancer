Male Breast Cancer

While breast cancer is often thought of as a women's disease, men can develop it too. Learn about the unique aspects of male breast cancer, including risk factors, symptoms, and treatment options.

Overview

Male breast cancer is a rare disease that forms in the breast tissue of men. Although men don't have breasts like women, they do have a small amount of breast tissue that can develop cancer.

Common Myths and Facts

Myth

Only women can get breast cancer.

Fact

Men have breast tissue and can develop breast cancer, though it's less common than in women.

Myth

Male breast cancer only affects older men.

Fact

While more common in older men, breast cancer can affect men of any age.

Risk Factors

Several factors can increase a man's risk of developing breast cancer:

Genetic Factors

  • BRCA1 or BRCA2 gene mutations
  • Family history of breast cancer
  • Klinefelter syndrome

Hormonal Factors

  • Elevated estrogen levels
  • Hormone therapy for prostate cancer
  • Liver disease

Lifestyle Factors

  • Obesity
  • Heavy alcohol consumption
  • Lack of exercise

Signs and Symptoms

Common symptoms of male breast cancer include:

  • A painless lump or thickening in breast tissue
  • Changes in the skin covering the breast
  • Changes in the nipple, including redness or scaling
  • Nipple discharge
  • Nipple retraction (turning inward)
  • Swollen lymph nodes under the arm

Diagnosis

Diagnosing male breast cancer typically involves:

  • Clinical breast examination
  • Diagnostic mammogram
  • Breast ultrasound
  • Biopsy
  • Genetic testing (if appropriate)

Treatment Options

Treatment for male breast cancer may include:

Surgery

Usually modified radical mastectomy due to the small amount of male breast tissue.

Radiation

Often used after surgery to reduce the risk of recurrence.

Chemotherapy

May be recommended before or after surgery, depending on the cancer stage.

Hormone Therapy

Often used as most male breast cancers are hormone receptor-positive.

Support and Resources

Being diagnosed with male breast cancer can be particularly challenging due to its rarity and the misconception that it's only a women's disease. We offer specialized support services and resources for men with breast cancer.
